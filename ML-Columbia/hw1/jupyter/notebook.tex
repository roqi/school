    All the other dimensions did not change very much as lambda approached
5000 except the 4th and 6th dimensions. They had considerably higher
magnitudes when lambda was close to 0 (maximum likelihood) and decreased
the more we penalized them for their magnitudes.

This tells us that without any bias and depending strictly on the
model's maximum likelihood, those two dimensions are seen as very
influencial. I.E. the bigger the car weight and/or the smaller the car
year, the lower the miles per gallon of the car. Obviously our RR
assumption (prior) is that no single feature should have so much
influence therefore lambda's increase brings those features' magnitudes
down.
